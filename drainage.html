How Waste Water Leaves the Property

Drainage systems in Marlow homes are designed to carry used water away from sinks, baths, showers, toilets and appliances. Waste water flows through internal pipework and into the external drainage network, eventually connecting to the local sewer system.

The system relies on gravity, pipe gradients and venting to keep water moving smoothly and prevent odours from entering the home.

The Role of Traps

Every sink, basin, bath and appliance has a trap — a curved section of pipe that holds a small amount of water. This water forms a seal that prevents sewer gases from travelling back into the property.

Common trap types include:

  • P-traps – used under sinks and basins
  • U-traps – often found under baths
  • Bottle traps – compact traps used where space is limited

Internal Drainage Pipework

Internal waste pipes carry water from fixtures to the main soil stack or external drain. These pipes are usually plastic in modern homes, though older properties may have mixed materials.

Good drainage relies on correct pipe gradients. If the slope is too shallow, water may move slowly. If it is too steep, water may outrun solids, increasing the chance of blockages.

The Soil Stack

The soil stack is the main vertical pipe that carries waste from toilets and other fixtures to the underground drainage system. It is normally vented at the top to allow air movement, which helps maintain smooth flow and prevents pressure build-up.

External Drainage

Once waste water leaves the property, it flows into underground pipes that connect to the local sewer network. These pipes rely on gravity and correct alignment to maintain flow.

External drains may include inspection chambers, which allow access for maintenance and clearing blockages.

Typical Patterns Seen in Marlow

  • Slower drainage in older properties with original pipework
  • Gurgling sounds caused by air movement or partial blockages
  • Occasional odours if traps dry out or venting is restricted
  • Surface drains affected by leaves or debris during wet seasons
