Neighbourhood Plumbing Terms

This glossary explains localised plumbing terms and characteristics that relate specifically to Marlow’s neighbourhoods.

  • Marlow Bottom Pressure Zone: A higher elevation area where water pressure is naturally lower due to distance and height above the main supply.
  • Riverside Drainage: Drainage systems located close to the River Thames, often affected by high water tables and seasonal moisture.
  • Clay Drainage Belt: Areas of Marlow with clay-heavy soil, where drains may be more prone to movement, cracking, or slower water flow.
  • Mixed Housing Stock: Neighbourhoods with a combination of older cottages, mid‑century homes, and modern builds — each with different plumbing systems.
  • High Water Table Zone: Areas such as Bisham and Lower Marlow where groundwater levels are naturally higher, affecting drainage performance.
  • Legacy Copper Systems: Older homes in central Marlow that still use traditional copper pipework, sometimes mixed with newer materials.
  • Modern Plastic Systems: Newer developments using plastic pipework throughout, offering improved flow and resistance to corrosion.
  • Shared Drainage Routes: Common in older terraced areas where multiple properties connect to the same drainage line.
  • Chalk Groundwater Zone: Areas built on chalk, where natural drainage is good but water hardness is high due to mineral content.
