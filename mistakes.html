Common Plumbing Mistakes to Avoid

Simple errors that can lead to leaks, blockages, or long‑term plumbing issues in homes across Marlow.

1. Pouring Grease or Oil Down the Sink

Grease may look like a liquid when hot, but it solidifies inside pipes. This is one of the most common causes of kitchen blockages in Marlow homes.

Better Alternative

Let grease cool, then dispose of it in the bin.

2. Using Chemical Drain Cleaners Too Often

While they may offer a quick fix, chemical drain cleaners can damage older pipework — especially clay or copper systems found in many Marlow properties.

Better Alternative

Use a plunger, drain snake, or preventative habits instead.

3. Ignoring Early Warning Signs

Slow drainage, gurgling sounds, or reduced water pressure often indicate developing issues. Ignoring them can lead to more serious problems later.

Common Warning Signs

  • Water draining slowly
  • Bad smells from sinks or drains
  • Reduced flow from taps or showers
  • Toilets taking longer to refill

4. Over‑Tightening Tap or Pipe Fittings

Over‑tightening can crack washers, damage threads, or distort seals — leading to leaks rather than preventing them.

Better Alternative

Hand‑tighten first, then gently secure with tools if needed.

5. Using the Wrong Tools for DIY Repairs

Incorrect tools can damage fittings or cause leaks. This is especially true in older homes with mixed pipe materials.

Examples

  • Using grips instead of a proper wrench
  • Forcing plastic fittings with metal tools
  • Using sealants where washers are required

6. Flushing Non‑Flushable Items

Wet wipes, cotton pads, and sanitary products can cause blockages — especially in older clay drainage systems common in Marlow.

Only Flush

Toilet paper and human waste.

7. Not Knowing Where the Stop Valve Is

In an emergency, every second counts. Many homeowners don’t know where their main stop valve is located.

Typical Locations

  • Under the kitchen sink
  • In a utility cupboard
  • Outside near the property boundary
