Why Maintenance Matters

Regular plumbing maintenance helps prevent leaks, blockages, and water damage. In Marlow, where hard water and mixed property ages are common, simple habits can make a big difference in keeping systems running smoothly.

1. Check Your Stop Valve

Every homeowner should know where the main stop valve is located. It is usually found:

  • Under the kitchen sink
  • In a utility cupboard
  • Outside near the property boundary

Turn it occasionally to prevent it from seizing.

2. Reduce Limescale Buildup

Marlow’s very hard water means limescale forms quickly. To reduce buildup:

  • Clean taps and showerheads regularly
  • Use descaling products on kettles and appliances
  • Check aerators if flow reduces

3. Keep Drains Flowing

Most blockages can be prevented with simple habits:

  • Avoid pouring grease or oil down sinks
  • Use drain strainers to catch hair and food waste
  • Run hot water after washing up to help clear residue

4. Protect Pipes in Winter

Cold weather can cause pipes to freeze, especially in lofts, garages, and outbuildings. To reduce risk:

  • Insulate exposed pipework
  • Keep heating on low during very cold nights
  • Open loft hatches slightly to allow warm air to circulate

5. Maintain Hot Water Systems

Hot water systems benefit from regular checks:

  • Bleed radiators if they feel cold at the top
  • Check boiler pressure periodically
  • Listen for unusual noises from cylinders or boilers

Any work on boilers or gas appliances must be carried out by qualified professionals.

6. Inspect Outdoor Drainage

Leaves, debris, and weather conditions can affect outdoor drains:

  • Clear leaves from gullies in autumn
  • Check for standing water after heavy rain
  • Ensure drain covers are secure
