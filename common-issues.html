1. Low Water Pressure

Low water pressure is one of the most common issues reported across Marlow. Several factors can contribute:

  • Limescale buildup: Hard water can narrow internal pipe diameters over time.
  • Partially closed stop valves: A simple but common cause.
  • Elevation: Higher areas such as Marlow Bottom may naturally experience lower pressure.
  • Older pipework: Legacy copper or mixed systems may restrict flow.

2. Slow or Gurgling Drains

Slow drainage often indicates a developing blockage or venting issue. In older parts of Marlow, clay drainage pipes can make this more common.

  • Grease buildup: A major cause in kitchen sinks.
  • Hair and soap residue: Common in bathroom drains.
  • Tree root ingress: More likely in older clay systems.
  • Venting issues: Can cause gurgling sounds as water flows.

3. Limescale Buildup

Marlow’s very hard water leads to noticeable limescale deposits throughout the home. Homeowners often report:

  • White or chalky marks on taps and showerheads
  • Kettles furring up quickly
  • Reduced flow from taps and showers
  • Reduced efficiency in hot water systems

Limescale is especially common in properties with older plumbing systems or unvented cylinders that have not been descaled regularly.

4. Seasonal Plumbing Issues

Marlow’s seasonal temperature changes can affect plumbing performance, particularly in older homes.

  • Frozen pipes: More common in uninsulated lofts and outbuildings.
  • Expansion noise: Pipes expanding and contracting during temperature swings.
  • Surface water drainage: Heavy rain can overwhelm older drainage systems.

5. Noisy Pipework

Noisy pipes are often reported in older Marlow properties with mixed plumbing systems.

  • Water hammer: Sudden pressure changes causing banging noises.
  • Loose pipe clips: Pipes moving when water flows.
  • Air in the system: Can cause tapping or vibrating sounds.
