Low or Uneven Water Pressure

Pressure can vary across Marlow due to elevation, distance from supply points, and local demand. Homes at higher ground levels may naturally experience lower pressure, while older pipework or internal restrictions can also influence flow.

Common patterns include:

  • Stronger flow on lower floors than upper floors
  • Reduced pressure during busy morning or evening periods
  • Uneven flow between rooms depending on pipe layout

Slow Drainage

Slow drainage is often related to pipe gradients, older pipework, or partial blockages. In some older properties, original drainage layouts may not match modern usage patterns, leading to slower flow in certain fixtures.

Typical signs include:

  • Water pooling in sinks, baths or showers
  • Gurgling sounds from traps or pipework
  • Intermittent slowdowns depending on usage

Limescale Build‑Up

Marlow’s very hard water means limescale is a common and expected pattern. It forms more quickly in hot water systems and on surfaces where water evaporates.

Common areas affected:

  • Taps, shower heads and metal fittings
  • Kettles, boilers and hot water cylinders
  • Glass screens, tiles and surfaces
  • Internal pipework over long periods

Temperature‑Related Flow Changes

Hot water outlets may show different flow characteristics compared to cold outlets. This can be due to limescale build‑up, pipe routing, or the behaviour of heating systems.

Typical patterns include:

  • Reduced flow from hot taps compared to cold
  • Changes in flow after long periods of heating
  • More visible limescale on hot water fittings

Occasional Odours

Odours can occur if traps dry out, if there is restricted venting, or if external drains are affected by debris. These issues are often intermittent and related to usage patterns or weather conditions.
