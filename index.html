Overview of Home Plumbing

Plumbing systems are essential for delivering clean water into a property and removing wastewater safely. A typical home plumbing system includes water supply pipes, drainage pipes, fixtures, valves, and venting systems.

This guide provides neutral, factual information based on publicly available resources such as WRAS (Water Regulations Advisory Scheme) and CIPHE (Chartered Institute of Plumbing & Heating Engineering).

How the Water Supply System Works

The water supply system brings fresh, pressurised water into the home. It typically includes:

  • Main stop valve
  • Cold water storage (in some homes)
  • Hot water cylinder or combi boiler
  • Pipework delivering water to taps, toilets, and appliances

Water pressure is regulated to ensure safe operation. More information can be found at Water UK.

Understanding the Drainage System

The drainage system removes wastewater through gravity-fed pipes. Key components include:

  • Waste pipes from sinks, baths, and appliances
