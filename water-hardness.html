Why Marlow Has Very Hard Water

Marlow and the wider Buckinghamshire region sit on chalk and limestone geology. As rainwater passes through these layers, it absorbs minerals such as calcium and magnesium. This naturally creates very hard water, which is common across the Thames Valley.

Hard water is not harmful to health, but it does influence how plumbing systems age and perform.

Common Signs of Hard Water in Marlow Homes

Homeowners across Marlow, Marlow Bottom, Little Marlow and Bisham often notice:

  • White or chalky deposits on taps and showerheads
  • Kettles furring up quickly
  • Reduced flow from taps and showers over time
  • Limescale marks on tiles, sinks and appliances
  • Hot water systems becoming less efficient

These effects are more noticeable in older homes with traditional copper pipework or older hot water cylinders.

How Hard Water Affects Plumbing Systems

Hard water can gradually reduce the performance of plumbing systems by:

  • Limescale buildup: Forms inside pipes, taps, showerheads and appliances.
  • Reduced hot water efficiency: Limescale can coat heating elements and cylinders.
  • Lower flow rates: Narrowed pipework reduces water flow over time.
  • More frequent maintenance: Appliances such as kettles and washing machines may require descaling.

Modern systems are designed to cope better, but hard water still affects all properties to some degree.

Local Variations Across Marlow

Although the entire region has hard water, some areas experience slightly different effects:

  • Marlow Bottom: Hard water combined with elevation can reduce flow.
  • Little Marlow: Older pipework may show more limescale buildup.
  • Bisham: Riverside moisture and older systems can amplify issues.
  • Newer estates: Modern pipework handles hard water better but still requires regular descaling.
