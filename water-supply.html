Understanding Water Supply in Marlow

A simple guide explaining how mains water reaches homes across Marlow and the surrounding area.

Home Overview Drainage Water Hardness Common Issues

How Water Reaches Your Home

Marlow’s water supply comes from the local mains network, which delivers treated water to properties through underground pipes. Once it reaches your home, it enters through the main stop valve and feeds your internal pipework.

Source: “Water enters the home through the main stop valve and feeds internal pipework.”

Main Stop Valve

The stop valve controls the flow of water into your home. Most Marlow properties have it located under the kitchen sink, in a utility room, or near the front boundary.

Source: “Most homes have the main stop valve under the kitchen sink or near the boundary.”

Mains Pressure

Mains pressure varies depending on elevation, distance from supply points, and local demand. Homes in higher areas such as Marlow Bottom may experience slightly lower pressure.

Source: “Elevation and distance from supply points can affect mains pressure.”

Internal Pipework

Once inside the property, water is distributed through copper or plastic pipes to taps, toilets, showers and appliances. Older homes may still have original pipework, which can affect flow and reliability.

Source: “Older homes may still have original pipework which can affect flow.”

Common Water Supply Issues

  • Low pressure caused by limescale or partially closed valves
  • Airlocks in older systems
  • Worn or corroded pipework reducing flow
  • Hard water affecting taps and appliances

Source: “Low pressure… airlocks… worn pipework… hard water.”

When to Seek Professional Help

If you experience persistent low pressure, leaks, or inconsistent flow, a qualified professional can assess the system and ensure everything meets current safety standards.
