Understanding Mains Water in Marlow

Most homes in Marlow receive mains water from the local water authority through underground supply pipes. Pressure and flow can vary depending on elevation, distance from the main supply, and the age of the property’s internal pipework.

Buckinghamshire, including Marlow, is known for very hard water. Hard water contains higher levels of dissolved minerals such as calcium and magnesium, which can contribute to limescale buildup in taps, showerheads, kettles and hot water systems.

Main Components of the Water Supply System

Main Stop Valve

Usually located under the kitchen sink, in a utility area, or near the property boundary. Knowing where this valve is located is essential for emergencies.

Cold Water Storage Tank

Common in older homes, typically found in loft spaces. Modern homes often use mains-fed systems without storage tanks.

Hot Water Cylinder or Combi Boiler

Hot water cylinders store heated water, while combi boilers heat water on demand. Both systems are widely used across Marlow.

Distribution Pipework

Pipework carries cold and hot water to taps, baths, showers and appliances. Older homes may still use copper pipework, while newer builds use modern plastic systems.

How Hard Water Affects the Water Supply

Marlow’s hard water can lead to:

  • Reduced flow from taps and showers
  • Limescale buildup in kettles and appliances
  • White or chalky deposits on taps and showerheads
  • Reduced efficiency in hot water systems

Hard water does not pose a health risk, but it can influence how plumbing systems age and perform. Homes with older pipework may notice more pronounced effects.

Why Water Pressure Varies Across Marlow

Water pressure can differ from one neighbourhood to another due to:

  • Elevation: Higher areas such as Marlow Bottom may experience lower pressure.
  • Distance from the main supply: Homes further from supply points may notice reduced flow.
  • Internal pipework: Limescale buildup or partially closed valves can restrict flow.
  • Property age: Older systems may not meet modern pressure expectations.
