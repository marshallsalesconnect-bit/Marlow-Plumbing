How Water Reaches the Property

Homes in Marlow receive treated mains water through underground supply pipes that form part of the local distribution network. These pipes deliver water to the boundary of each property, where an external stop tap or valve is usually located. This valve allows the supply to be isolated if needed.

The Main Stop Valve Inside the Home

Once water enters the building, it passes through an internal main stop valve. This valve controls the flow of water into the home’s internal pipework. It is commonly found under the kitchen sink, in a utility area, or near the point where the pipe enters the property.

Being able to locate and operate this valve is useful for managing leaks or carrying out maintenance.

Internal Pipework and Distribution

From the main stop valve, water is distributed through internal pipework to taps, toilets, showers and appliances. The materials used can vary depending on the age of the property. Older homes may have copper or mixed materials, while newer homes often use modern plastics.

Some properties supply all cold outlets directly from the mains. Others, especially older homes, may have storage tanks or cylinders that supply certain fixtures.

What Affects Water Pressure?

Elevation and distance

Marlow’s varied ground levels mean that properties at higher elevations may naturally experience lower pressure than those at lower points.

Pipe condition and layout

Internal restrictions, older pipework, or complex routing can influence how water flows to different outlets within the home.

Local demand

Pressure can vary during peak usage times, such as early mornings or evenings, when many households are using water at the same time.

Typical Patterns Seen in Marlow

  • Stronger pressure on lower floors compared to upper floors
  • Variations in flow during busy periods
  • Reduced flow in older pipework or fittings affected by limescale
  • Differences in pressure between rooms depending on layout
