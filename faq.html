Frequently Asked Questions

This page answers common questions about how plumbing systems work in Marlow and what households typically experience.

Questions & Answers

Why is the water in Marlow so hard?

Marlow sits on chalk and limestone geology. As rainwater passes through these layers, it absorbs minerals such as calcium and magnesium, resulting in very hard water.

Is hard water safe to drink?

Yes. Hard water is safe to drink and use. The minerals that cause hardness are naturally occurring.

Why do I see limescale on taps and shower heads?

When hard water evaporates or is heated, minerals solidify and form limescale. This is common in hot water outlets and on surfaces where water dries.

Why does my water pressure vary?

Pressure can vary due to elevation, distance from supply points, internal pipework layout, and peak usage times. Homes at higher ground levels may naturally experience lower pressure.

Why is my hot water flow weaker than my cold water flow?

Hot water systems often experience more limescale build‑up, especially in older pipework or appliances. This can reduce flow over time.

Why does my sink or bath drain slowly?

Slow drainage can be caused by partial blockages, older pipework, or shallow gradients. Gurgling sounds may indicate restricted air movement in the system.

What is the main stop valve and where is it?

The main stop valve controls the flow of water into the home. It is often located under the kitchen sink, in a utility area, or near where the supply pipe enters the property.

Why do I sometimes hear gurgling noises?

Gurgling is usually caused by air moving through the drainage system. It can occur when water displaces air or when there is a partial blockage.

Why do I occasionally notice odours?

Odours can occur if traps dry out, if venting is restricted, or if external drains are affected by debris. These issues are often intermittent.
