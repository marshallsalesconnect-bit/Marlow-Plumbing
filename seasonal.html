Winter: Freezing Pipes & Low Temperatures

Cold weather can cause water inside pipes to freeze, especially in lofts, garages, and external walls. Frozen pipes may expand and split, leading to leaks once they thaw.

Common Winter Issues

  • Frozen outdoor taps and exposed pipework
  • Reduced flow from cold taps in the morning
  • Condensation around cold pipes
  • Higher strain on boilers and heating systems

Prevention Tips

  • Insulate exposed pipes
  • Keep heating on low during very cold nights
  • Open loft hatches slightly to allow warm air to circulate

Spring: Ground Movement & Increased Rainfall

As temperatures rise and the ground shifts, older clay drainage systems may experience movement or cracking. Spring rain can also highlight issues with outdoor drainage.

Common Spring Issues

  • Tree root ingress into older drains
  • Shifting soil affecting pipe alignment
  • Overflowing gullies after heavy rain

Summer: High Water Usage

Hot weather and increased outdoor activity can place extra demand on water systems. Homes in higher areas such as Marlow Bottom may notice temporary pressure drops.

Common Summer Issues

  • Lower water pressure during peak usage
  • Increased limescale buildup due to higher water temperatures
  • Outdoor taps and hoses stressing older pipework

Autumn: Leaves & Drainage Blockages

Falling leaves and wetter weather can affect outdoor drainage systems, especially around driveways, patios, and garden gullies.

Common Autumn Issues

  • Blocked gullies and surface drains
  • Standing water after rainfall
  • Debris entering outdoor pipework
