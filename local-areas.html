Why Plumbing Varies Across Marlow

Marlow’s neighbourhoods differ in elevation, soil type, property age and proximity to the River Thames. These factors influence water pressure, drainage performance, and the likelihood of certain plumbing issues.

Marlow Bottom

Marlow Bottom sits at a higher elevation than the town centre, which affects water pressure and drainage behaviour.

Typical Characteristics

  • Lower mains water pressure due to elevation
  • Modern housing developments with plastic pipework
  • Longer pipe runs from the main supply

Common Issues

  • Reduced flow from taps and showers
  • Airlocks in long pipe runs
  • Hard water causing limescale buildup

Little Marlow

Little Marlow includes older cottages, rural properties and some modern homes. Soil conditions and property age influence plumbing performance.

Typical Characteristics

  • Mixed clay and chalk soil affecting drainage
  • Older pipework in traditional homes
  • Some properties with private drainage systems

Common Issues

  • Slower drainage in clay-heavy areas
  • Tree root ingress into older clay drains
  • Limescale buildup in older copper systems

Bisham

Bisham’s riverside location and older housing stock create unique plumbing characteristics.

Typical Characteristics

  • High water table due to proximity to the Thames
  • Older drainage systems in historic properties
  • Moisture affecting external pipework

Common Issues

  • Slower drainage during wet weather
  • Increased risk of standing water outdoors
  • Potential for ground movement affecting older drains

Central Marlow

The town centre includes Victorian terraces, mid‑century homes and modern apartments, each with different plumbing characteristics.

Typical Characteristics

  • Older properties with traditional copper pipework
  • Mixed drainage systems (clay and plastic)
  • Higher demand on shared drainage routes

Common Issues

  • Lower flow in older pipework
  • Recurring blockages in shared drainage lines
  • Limescale affecting taps and appliances
