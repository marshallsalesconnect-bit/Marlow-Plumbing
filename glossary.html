Plumbing Terms Explained

This glossary provides clear explanations of terms commonly used when discussing household plumbing systems in Marlow and the surrounding Buckinghamshire area.

  • Stop Valve: A valve that shuts off the main water supply to the property. Often located under the kitchen sink or near the property boundary.
  • Soil Stack: A vertical pipe that carries waste from toilets to the underground drainage system.
  • P-Trap / U-Bend: A curved pipe section that holds water to block sewer gases from entering the home.
  • Vent Pipe: A pipe that allows air into the drainage system to maintain correct pressure and flow.
  • Limescale: A chalky mineral deposit caused by hard water, commonly found on taps, showerheads and inside appliances.
  • Mains Pressure: The pressure of water supplied from the local water authority.
  • Gravity Drainage: A system where wastewater flows downward through pipes using gravity rather than pumps.
  • Cold Water Storage Tank: A tank typically found in older homes, usually located in the loft, used to store cold water.
  • Combi Boiler: A boiler that heats water on demand without using a separate hot water cylinder.
  • Hot Water Cylinder: A tank that stores heated water for use in taps, baths and showers.
  • Waste Pipe: A pipe that carries wastewater from sinks, baths, showers and appliances.
  • Airlock: A blockage caused by trapped air in a water pipe, often affecting hot water flow.
