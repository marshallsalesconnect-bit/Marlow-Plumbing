Glossary

This glossary explains key plumbing terms used across the site. It is designed to help readers understand how different parts of a plumbing system work and how they relate to each other.

Terms and Definitions

Main Stop Valve

The internal valve that controls the flow of mains water into the property. Often located under the kitchen sink or near the entry point of the supply pipe.

External Stop Tap

A valve located at the property boundary that can isolate the water supply from the mains network.

Trap

A curved section of pipe that holds water to prevent sewer gases from entering the home. Found under sinks, basins, baths and appliances.

Soil Stack

The main vertical pipe that carries waste from toilets and other fixtures to the underground drainage system. Usually vented at the top.

Vent Pipe

A pipe that allows air to enter the drainage system, helping maintain smooth flow and preventing pressure build‑up.

Limescale

A hard, chalky deposit formed when minerals in hard water (such as calcium and magnesium) solidify. Common in kettles, boilers, taps and shower heads.

Hard Water

Water with high mineral content. Marlow is in a very hard water area due to local geology.

Flow Rate

The amount of water that passes through a pipe or outlet over time. Affected by pressure, pipe condition and layout.

Pressure

The force that pushes water through pipes. Influenced by elevation, demand, and the condition of the supply network.

Gradient

The slope of a drainage pipe. Correct gradients help waste water flow smoothly.

Inspection Chamber

An access point in the external drainage system used for maintenance and clearing blockages.

Cold Mains

The direct supply of cold water from the mains network to fixtures such as kitchen taps and toilets.

Hot Water Cylinder

A tank that stores heated water for use in taps and showers. More common in older or traditional heating systems.

Combi Boiler

A boiler that heats water on demand without storing it. Common in modern homes.
